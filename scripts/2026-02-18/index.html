A* frontier-level diagnostics for pax routing

This page documents a diagnostic re-instrumentation pass focused on search behavior. The goal is to expose frontier evolution, open and closed set transitions, and pruning events. It does not change routing policy for optimization.

What was instrumented

  • Expanded node and its g, h, f values per step
  • Frontier size and nodes added to open set
  • Nodes skipped or pruned during expansion
  • Closed set membership changes
  • Best-path candidate evolution

Observed findings

  • All three variants found a route from Grand Central to Carnegie Hall.
  • First search divergence occurred at step 2 for both stress-weighted variants.
  • Nodes explored: baseline 57, paper heuristic 433, stress-weighted heuristic 446.
  • No sustained frontier-collapse event was detected under the 10% of peak frontier threshold criterion.
  • Baseline-path nodes were not permanently pruned before any expansion in the weighted variants.

Why frontier visibility matters for admissibility checks

Final path outputs do not show whether candidate states were dropped before evaluation. Frontier traces reveal when pruning is due to closed-set policy, stale entries, or objective reweighting. This visibility is required to confirm or rule out premature elimination of admissible alternatives.
